**Project Description:**
The Stress & Study Balance Tracker is a mental health support system for students to combat academic stress and adopt a better study routine. Students face various pressures in their assignments, examinations, and time management. Academic stress can have a detrimental impact on their mental health.

This system will enable the students to keep track of the amount of time spent studying and also keep track of their stress levels, hence becoming more conscious of their academic pressures and well-being. The system will also provide the students with AI recommendations and relaxation techniques, such as taking breaks and practicing breathing exercises, among others.
 
The system has features such as study hour tracking, stress level monitoring, relaxation techniques, and time management reminders. Through the use of these tools, the student will be able to better organize their study plans. This project mainly helps students who face various academic pressures. In particular, university students face various difficulties in dealing with academic pressures as well as maintaining a healthy state of mind.


**Overview of Your SDLC Work:**
We used the software development life cycle to design our mental health support system. In the planning phase, we linked our project with the third SDG and identified our stakeholders and created a product backlog. In the analysis and design phase, we determined our functional requirements and created our mental health support system using use cases and class diagrams, as well as UI design.

In the implementation phase, we created a functional prototype with basic tools to track studies and monitor stress levels. In the testing and evaluation phase, we carried out a formal test plan and obtained feedback to improve our design. We created an agile process using a detailed report and organized our work using GitHub and Trello to submit our work.
